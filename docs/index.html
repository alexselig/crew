Why Crew

Never lose track of which agent needs you.

Juggling five terminals means five things to babysit. Crew watches them all and only interrupts you when it matters.

⌨︎

It owns the terminals

Crew spawns each agent's PTY, so it sees the full output stream — no fragile scraping of window titles or logs.

🚦

Working vs. waiting-for-you

A detection engine tells a busy agent apart from one blocked on your input, guarded against false alarms during think-time.

🔴

A red dot when it needs you

Menu-bar badge with a live count, jump-to-waiting, and a native notification the instant a session starts waiting.

🦊

Characters & labels

Every session gets a distinct animal and an editable name, remembered by project so relaunching a job reuses its identity.

✈️

Autopilot-aware

When an agent runs autonomously — Claude's accept-edits or Copilot's autopilot — its character puts on a pilot costume.

▦

Grid, groups & workspaces

Flip to a live dashboard of every session, group by status or project, and filter into named workspaces.

How it works

Launch. Walk away. Get pinged.

1

Launch your agents

Start Claude Code, Copilot CLI, a shell, or any custom command + working directory — each in its own owned session.

2

Crew watches the streams

It reads every PTY, animates the characters while they work, and flips a session to a red dot the moment it's blocked on you.

3

Jump to whoever needs you

Click the menu-bar badge to leap straight to the waiting session, answer it, and let the crew keep working.
